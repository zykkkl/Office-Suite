# Work Reporting

> Domain: Performance reviews, quarterly/annual reports, project retrospectives, government/party reports, achievement presentations
> Cognitive posture: Scanning-then-reading -- leadership scans for key numbers first, then reads supporting detail

## Cognitive Basis

Work reports serve a dual audience function:

1. **Scanning layer**: Senior leadership flips through rapidly, spending 3-5 seconds per slide to extract key metrics and conclusions
2. **Reading layer**: Detailed reviewers dig into supporting evidence, process descriptions, and data sources

The design must serve both layers simultaneously. The scanning layer is served by large, bold conclusion titles and KPI numbers. The reading layer is served by structured body content with complete data.

## Density Target: Dense (70-80%)

Each slide answers one performance question. Minimum content per slide:
- One conclusion sentence (the title)
- 2-3 KPI numbers with comparison benchmarks
- 4-5 supporting evidence points
- At least one chart or structured graphic

## Visual-Verbal Ratio

Approximately 60% text and tables, 30% charts and structured graphics, 10% icons and spatial structure.

Charts are mandatory for any quantified achievement. Presenting numbers without visual comparison (planned vs actual, current vs prior period) is analytically incomplete.

Structured graphics (flowcharts, organizational diagrams, process maps) replace text lists for any multi-step or relational content.

## Color Derivation

Temperature: neutral-professional. The palette should project competence and objectivity without emotional coloring.

| Token | Default | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| accent | Brand color or a measured warm tone | Used sparingly for KPI highlights and progress indicators |
| bg_primary | White `#FFFFFF` | Maximum readability for dense content |

For government/party contexts: the accent may shift to red/gold tones aligned with institutional identity. Content pages remain white/light for readability.

## Typography Posture

Every slide title is an **achievement sentence** that quantifies the result:

Wrong: "Sales Performance"
Right: "Q3 Sales Revenue Up 23% YoY, Exceeding Annual Target by 8%"

KPI numbers displayed at 34pt+ with the accent color, accompanied by comparison benchmarks (YoY%, QoQ%, completion rate). Numbers without reference points are meaningless.

## Content Structure

Standard slide anatomy:

```
Navigation indicator (optional)
  |
Title: Achievement sentence (H1, accent or bold)
  |
Divider line (thin, accent color)
  |
Content zone:
  +-- KPI cards (2-3 big numbers with benchmarks)
  +-- Evidence bullets (4-5 quantified points)
  +-- Chart or structured graphic (1 per slide minimum)
  |
Next step / action item (if applicable)
  |
Page marker (bottom-right)
```

## Structured Graphics Usage

| Content Type | Visual Strategy |
|-------------|----------------|
| Work process / workflow | Flowchart (shapes + connectors + labels) |
| Organizational structure | Hierarchy diagram |
| Improvement cycle (PDCA) | Cycle diagram |
| Multi-factor analysis | Matrix or relationship diagram |
| Sales pipeline / conversion | Funnel diagram |
| Planned vs actual | Comparison table with completion rates |
| Timeline / milestones | Horizontal timeline with markers |

Every structured graphic replaces a text list, never supplements one.

## Narrative Convention

Logic flow: Achievement -> Process -> Next Steps.

Each slide leads with results, not process. Leadership wants to know WHAT was achieved before HOW it was done.

Problems and challenges must be paired with proposed solutions. Raising issues without action plans is incomplete reporting.

## Prohibited Patterns

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Qualitative achievements without numbers | "Significant improvement" is unverifiable |
| Charts without comparison benchmarks | A number in isolation has no meaning |
| Pure text lists for process content | Structured graphics are faster to scan |
| More than 4 colors | Professional restraint projects competence |
| Decorative imagery | Every pixel must serve information delivery |
| Text effects (shadow, glow) | Standard bold is sufficient for emphasis |
