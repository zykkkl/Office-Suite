# Education and Training

> Domain: Classroom teaching (K-12, university), corporate training, student presentations, educational lectures
> Cognitive posture: Schema-building -- the audience is constructing new mental models, not evaluating existing ones

## Cognitive Load Management

Education presentations must actively manage three types of cognitive load (Sweller, 1988):

| Load Type | Definition | Design Response |
|-----------|-----------|----------------|
| **Intrinsic** | Complexity inherent to the topic | Chunk information into single-concept slides |
| **Extraneous** | Complexity caused by poor design | Eliminate decorative noise, maintain consistent layout |
| **Germane** | Effort devoted to learning | Support with visual metaphors, comparisons, worked examples |

The design's primary job is to minimize extraneous load, freeing the audience's working memory for intrinsic and germane processing.

## Audience-Calibrated Parameters

| Parameter | K-6 (6-12 yrs) | Secondary (13-17 yrs) | University | Corporate Training |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|------------|-------------------|
| Concepts per slide | 1 | 1-2 | 1-3 | 1-3 |
| Density | Sparse (40-55%) | Balanced (55-65%) | Dense (65-80%) | Dense (65-80%) |
| Visual metaphor need | High | Moderate | Low | Moderate |
| Text volume | Minimal | Moderate | Standard | Standard |
| Font minimum | 18pt | 14pt | 12pt | 12pt |

## Visual-Verbal Ratio

Early education: 20% text, 60% visuals/illustrations, 20% whitespace.
Secondary: 40% text, 40% diagrams/charts, 20% whitespace.
University/corporate: 45% text, 40% charts/diagrams, 15% whitespace.

Diagrams are not decoration in education -- they are comprehension scaffolds. Every abstract concept should have a visual representation.

## Color Derivation

Temperature: warm-accessible for younger audiences, cool-focused for professional audiences.

| Token | Default | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| accent | Topic-appropriate semantic color | Can encode knowledge categories (blue=concept, green=example, orange=caution) |
| bg_primary | White or very light for content; dark acceptable for chapter/cover pages | Projection readability is non-negotiable |

Maximum 4 semantic colors (excluding grayscale). Each color has a consistent meaning across the deck.

## Typography Posture

Page titles carry knowledge-point conclusions, not chapter labels.

Wrong: "Photosynthesis"
Right: "Plants Convert Sunlight into Chemical Energy Through Two Stages"

Projection readability is mandatory. Test all font sizes and color contrasts against worst-case projection conditions (low contrast, ambient light).

## Pedagogical Sequencing

Each topic follows: **Anchor** (connect to prior knowledge) -> **Explain** (new concept) -> **Exemplify** (concrete case) -> **Check** (comprehension question or summary).

This sequence maps to 2-4 slides per topic depending on complexity.

## Content Expression

| Concept Type | Visual Strategy |
|-------------|----------------|
| Process/workflow | Shape + connector flowchart |
| Comparison | Side-by-side card grid or table |
| Cause-effect | Left-right split with arrow |
| Classification | Hierarchical shape diagram |
| Timeline | Horizontal node sequence |
| Right vs wrong | Paired cards with visual indicators (green/red) |

## Prohibited Patterns

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---------|-------------|
| Dense text walls (>6 consecutive lines) | Exceeds working memory capacity |
| Decorative images unrelated to content | Consumes spatial budget without aiding comprehension |
| Font sizes below 12pt (or 18pt for K-6) | Unreadable in classroom projection |
| Large dark-background content pages | Reduces chart and text legibility under projection |
| Pure text concept definitions | Eliminates the visual processing channel |
| Inconsistent layout across concept slides | Increases extraneous cognitive load |
